🛒 Job Description – Shopfloor Assistant

Sales Assistant · Moriarty's SuperValu · Skerries

Main Purpose of the Role

To provide excellent services and first class customer care through I-Care practices. To support the store's management team in delivering the "Real Food Real People" brand through efficient work practices and team work. The Shopfloor Assistant role involves merchandising and rotating stock in the grocery department, handling and recording of all customer queries and complaints and informing customers of the various services, products and promotions on offer.

Main Duties

  1. To ensure that all sections of the grocery department are merchandised and presented to the highest standard
  2. To wear full uniform, name badge and display a neat appearance at all times
  3. To provide a friendly, efficient and polite service to all our customers at all times
  4. To adhere to all company rules, policies and procedures at all times (per the handbook and policy boards)
  5. Ensure that any product going on the shelf has a SEL or is stocked in front of the appropriate SEL
  6. To attend any training that has been scheduled for you
  7. To be familiar with all price promotions and continuity promotions and be able to explain and promote such to customers
  8. To assist customers to pack shopping bags at checkouts as required
  9. To provide a trolley to car service when requested
  10. To ensure you take note of your roster correctly so as not to miss or be late for any shifts
  11. Report any discrepancies and problems to Managers
  12. Maintaining hygiene and food safety standards on the Shopfloor
  13. To ensure you clock appropriately for every shift/break/lunch
  14. To carry out any other duties as requested by management/supervisors from time to time

Customer Care

  1. Approach customers and ask if they need any help
  2. To greet customers in a genuine, friendly and welcoming manner
  3. To give the customer your full attention and stay engaged throughout the interaction
  4. If a customer asks where something is, take them to it (do not send them searching for the product)
  5. Engage in a polite and friendly conversation
  6. Always ensure you ask the customer if they need anything else once you've answered their query
  7. Offer additional advice or information on products/offers
  8. Always end the interaction with a thank you and goodbye

Health & Safety

  1. Always adhere to the Slip/Trip and Falls directive and never leave a spillage unattended
  2. Adhere to all Safety instructions given and safety notices displayed
  3. Never run while in the building as this can cause accidents
  4. Horseplay is not tolerated as your actions could result in injury to yourself, your colleagues or customers
  5. In the event of a fire please exit through your nearest exit and proceed to the recycling bins at the far end of the car park
  6. Always inform your manager if you need to leave your work station
  7. Report any defective equipment to management and do not use it
  8. Be security conscious at all times
  9. Always get your receipt signed by security personnel or supervisor after each purchase
  10. Always pull a combi when transporting stock
Note: This job description is neither definitive nor restrictive and it may be modified to meet changing needs.

🔞 Alcohol, Tobacco, Vapes & Paracetamol

Priority Training Directive · HR Dept 2025

Important: Staff will be held accountable for all sales they process through the tills. Breaches of this legislation will be dealt with by the Gardaí, who will not only hold the store accountable but the sales assistant also.

Sale of Alcohol

  1. Only staff who are 18 years of age or over may actually sell alcohol in our store
  2. If you are under 18 and a customer asks you to take a sale with alcohol in it — you must call your supervisor immediately
  3. Only persons aged 18 years of age or over can legally purchase alcohol
  4. Anyone who looks under 23 years of age should be asked for photographic ID to show proof of age
  5. The preferred proof of age ID is the Garda National Age Card (a passport or driving licence may also be accepted)
  6. Ensure that the photo on the ID is the actual person in front of you
  7. If a customer cannot produce ID — refuse the sale politely and explain they may return with appropriate ID
  8. If a customer becomes difficult, seek the assistance of your supervisor/manager immediately
  9. If you suspect alcohol is being purchased for someone under 18, consult your supervisor before processing the sale
  10. Never accept payment for alcohol from a minor — even if they are with an adult
  11. Do not sell alcohol to an individual who is heavily intoxicated
  12. Report any suspicious behaviour to your supervisor who will record it in the incident book
Very Important: Employees who sell alcohol to underage drinkers can themselves be prosecuted for "aiding and abetting." An Garda Síochána are now prosecuting employees involved in underage sales as well as the licence holders themselves.

Sale of Tobacco

  1. Only staff who are 18 years of age or over may sell tobacco/tobacco products in our store
  2. If you are under 18 and a customer asks you to take a sale with tobacco in it — you must call your supervisor immediately
  3. Only people 18 years of age or over are legally entitled to purchase tobacco products
  4. If you suspect someone is under 18 (use under 23 as a guideline) ask to see photo ID
  5. If a customer cannot produce ID — refuse the sale politely
  6. If a customer becomes difficult, bring this to the attention of your supervisor immediately

Sale of E-Cigarettes (Vapes)

  1. Only staff who are 18 years of age or over may sell E-Cigarettes/Vapes or vaping products in our store
  2. If you are under 18 and a customer asks you to take a sale with vapes in it — you must call your supervisor immediately
  3. Only people 18 years of age or over are legally entitled to purchase E-Cigarettes/Vapes or vaping products
  4. If you suspect someone is under 18 (use under 23 as a guideline) ask to see photo ID
  5. If a customer cannot produce ID — refuse the sale politely
  6. If a customer becomes difficult, bring this to the attention of your supervisor immediately

Sale of Paracetamol

  1. Only 1 paracetamol-containing product may be sold in each retail transaction
  2. It is not acceptable to serve 1 customer 2 paracetamol products by scanning them in 2 separate transactions
  3. Your till is programmed to prompt you when a paracetamol product is scanned — you must remain vigilant
  4. "Contains Paracetamol" should be clearly marked on the outer packaging of relevant products
  5. This rule is LAW and not just our own internal policy
  6. Store policy: only sell paracetamol products to customers who are 18 years of age or over
  7. If a customer becomes annoyed, contact your Supervisor/Manager in a tactful/sensitive manner
Remember: The above rules are Law in Ireland. Spot checks are carried out by An Garda Síochána regarding alcohol, tobacco and vapes, and by the HPRA regarding Paracetamol products.

I understand that failure to adhere to the above will be treated as gross misconduct and managed through the company's disciplinary process, which could be up to and including dismissal.

🎲 Lotto Training Directive

Think 21 Campaign · HR Dept 2021

All retailers in conjunction with PLI (Operator of the National Lottery) are working together to ensure that Lotto/Lottery products such as scratch cards are not sold to anyone under the age of 18. The reason for this restriction is to help prevent potential problem gambling behaviours later in life.

As with alcohol and tobacco sales we are legally required to ask for a formal form of ID such as a Passport, Garda National Age Card or Drivers Licence to establish that an individual is 18 years of age or older before we can sell them Lotto, EuroMillions, any other lotto game and scratch cards.

Think 21: PLI have requested that we "Think 21" and ask any individual who looks like they could be under 21 years of age for proof of age if they wish to buy lotto/lotto products or wish to hand in a winning ticket.

With Immediate Effect

  1. Anyone who looks under 21 years of age is to be asked for photographic ID to show proof of age
  2. The preferred proof of age ID is the Garda National Age Card (a passport or driving licence may also be accepted)
  3. Ensure that the photo on the ID is the actual person in front of you
  4. If a customer who looks under 21 cannot produce an ID — do not feel under pressure to serve them. Politely explain that you are obliged to ask for proof of age and refuse the sale
  5. If a customer becomes difficult, seek the assistance of your supervisor/manager immediately
If in doubt about a person's age do not take any chances. It is better to decline a sale than mistakenly sell lottery/lotto products to someone who is under 18 years of age. Remember THINK 21!

💰 POS Awareness

Fake Notes, Money Cons & Till Security

Fake Money Tendered for Goods

Occasionally a customer will present a money note as payment for goods, and when the note is checked it will turn out to be a dud note. This may be done knowingly or unknowingly. The best procedure to follow:

  1. Check the note using the note check marker if available. The mark will indicate if the note is not real. Or carry out the various visual checks for watermarks, paper quality and stand out indicators. The most common recent indicator states along the short end of the note that it is "MOVIE MONEY" and "USED FOR MOTION PROPS"
  2. Politely make the customer aware that the note is not genuine and explain how you know this. The customer will most likely offer you another note
  3. Most customers will want you to return the note to them — do not return the note. Banks have given directives to retailers that fake notes should be taken out of circulation
  4. It is important not to draw attention to the customer who in most cases will be embarrassed

Money Con / Dupe Artists

The perpetrators are professionals. Their art is practiced to perfection. The act itself is based on your confusion, distraction and the quickness of their hands.
  1. They will only approach a checkout that has a large amount of cash in the drawer. They will watch till drawers open and close until they find one with an abundance of cash notes. They may work as couples or alone
  2. A small item will be purchased and then the person will look to change a large sum of €20 notes to €50 notes
  3. The person will switch €20s to €50s with you (normally around €400), then tell you they don't want the €50s anymore. You will give back all their €20 notes — but your till will be short €100/€150
  4. Do not keep excess cash in your till drawer. Request cash pick-ups from supervisors. If a request is made, politely close your till drawer and tell the person you will have to get a supervisor. They will most likely walk away

Not all money con transactions are carried out in the same manner. There have been recent cases where customers begin the transaction with a cash note and then change it for a different note in order to confuse the cashier. If you find yourself in this kind of situation it is VITAL that you close your till drawer immediately and advise that you must call a manager for authorization and assistance.

All staff must also be vigilant with cigarette and vape scams. When a customer requests cigarettes, always make sure that the transaction is paid for before cigarettes/vapes are handed over.

📚 Shopfloor SOP

Standard Operating Procedures – Moriarty's SuperValu

Welcome to the team! As a part of the team here in Moriarty's SuperValu, there are many duties that each of us carry out every day. This manual has been compiled especially for floor staff who play a very important part in the success of our store. Your Staff Manager/Manager will go through the manual in detail with you to ensure you understand its contents.

1. Uniform & Personal Presentation

Your uniform consists of: Black Trousers (no jeans or tracksuit bottoms), SuperValu Polo-shirt or Fleece, Black Shoes – polished (no trainers), Name Badge. Your Name Badge is as important as your shoes.

  • Always report to work in full uniform, which should be clean, neat & tidy
  • Hair should be neat and tidy — long hair should be tied back
  • Jewellery should be kept to a minimum
  • Be conscious of your personal hygiene — use an anti-perspirant deodorant
  • Any discrepancies in uniform must be reported to the Duty Manager before the start of your shift

2. Customer Service

  • Always smile and greet the customer with a simple hello
  • If a customer appears to be looking for something, ask if you can help
  • If a customer asks for an item not on the shelf, check the back stores or order it in through your manager
  • If a customer asks where something is, do not point — take them to the particular product
  • If a customer appears to be struggling with their basket or trolley, offer assistance
  • If a customer asks you a question, stop what you are doing and give them your full 100% attention
  • If a customer has a complaint, listen to them and then explain that you will get a manager

3. Label Discipline

Every product on a shelf must have a label (SEL — Shelf Edge Label) which corresponds to it. If a product does not have a barcode it must have a PLU number.

  • SEL Positioning: The label must always be located on the front of the product to the left hand side
  • 3 Digit Code Check: When packing shelves you must always carry out a 3 digit code check — check the last 3 digits on the product's barcode with the last 3 digits on the SEL. If they don't match, leave one unit in the scanning office so a new label can be produced
  • The 3 digit code check must be carried out on 1 item out of every case of stock — even stock you are familiar with, as product codes can change frequently
  • The Competition and Consumer Protection Commission can administer fines for EVERY product that doesn't have a label in front of it on the shelf

4. Merchandising Standards

  • Shelf Hygiene: Any spillages or marks on shelves should be dealt with before packing more stock
  • Stock Rotation: Always rotate your stock — the product with the shortest time left on its best before date should be pulled to the front so newer stock is packed at the back
  • Out of Stocks: Leave the allocated space empty. Use a "sorry, out of stock" label from the scanning office. Remove this label when the product is back in stock. At no time should a gap be filled with surrounding stock
  • Facing Off: Face the product in accordance with the amount of space/facings given to it. All products must have their label facing to the front. If there is not enough product to fill the space, leave a gap — do not fill with other products

5. Manual Handling / Lifting Procedures

8 Principles of Safe Lifting: (1) Assess the task, area & load, (2) Bend your knees, (3) Broad stable base — feet hip width apart, (4) Keep back straight, (5) Firm grip, (6) Keep arms close to the trunk, (7) Carry weight close to the centre of gravity, (8) Point/pivot feet in the direction of movement. Always use the combis, pallet trucks and hand trucks to move stock.

6. Slip & Trip Hazards

  • If you notice a spillage, stand over it immediately diverting people away. Clean up and place a yellow warning sign. Stay at the scene until the floor is dry. You never leave a spillage unattended!
  • Always keep your work area clear; keep all stock on combis until you are ready to pack it
  • Dispose of all empty boxes; remove combis and kick stools from the aisle when not in use
  • Do not walk past fallen stock — pick it up and return it to the shelf

7. Bag Packing

  • Ask the customer if you can pack their shopping and treat all items with care
  • Pack all non-food items together away from food
  • Keep all raw meat away from cooked/ready to eat food
  • Keep eggs, bread and other fragile products at the top
  • If the customer has a lot of bags or a trolley, offer assistance to their car

8. Home Deliveries

  • Pack bags into delivery crates — do not over pack
  • Obtain address from customer/cashier and fill details onto delivery sheet at customer service desk
  • Always double check the address and write a sticker for each crate

9. Combi Safety

  • Never overload a combi or pack stock in an unsecured way
  • When entering the shop floor, be at the front of the combi and check that the way is clear
  • Always secure combi before your journey by closing both sides
  • Always return to the stores area when you are finished

10. Health & Safety

  • Be conscious of the effect your actions will have on you and those around you
  • Adhere to safety procedures as set out by management and wear any protective clothing you are given
  • Report any defective equipment to management and do not use machinery you are not trained to use
  • Report any illness/accidents to management immediately

11. Floor Sweeping

  • Be mindful of customers always and clear all debris from the floor
  • Any loose stock is picked up and returned to shelf or returns area if damaged
  • All mats are clear and lying flat in place — straighten any folds
  • Sign off the floor sweeping schedule when you have completed your sweep

12. Returns / Damaged Stock

  • Always show/tell a member of management about any items you put in returns
  • Never put broken glass in returns and do not throw any stock in the bin unless instructed by a manager
  • There is a separate returns area for each of the fresh food departments

13. Packing of Fresh Foods

  • Stock rotation is the most important aspect — adhere to the "one date rule" where possible
  • In all circumstances where there is a doubt, consult the chargehand or manager for clarification

14. Procedure for Dead Stock

  • After all stock has been packed, place dead stock in the goods lift
  • Try to condense a few boxes onto existing dead stock combis already in the goods lift
  • If the lift is out of service or in use, show any dead stock combis to a member of management

15. Communication with Management

Report ALL issues that arise during your shift to a member of management, including: security issues, customer complaints, out of stocks, spillages, structural damage, conduct of other staff members, customer feedback.

16–20. Brand, Goods Inwards, Scanning, Checkouts & The Super/The Valu

All new staff will spend time with relevant team members to understand the Real Food Real People brand, goods inwards procedures, scanning office disciplines, basic till training, and the meaning behind "The Super The Valu — at SuperValu we do both."

I have received this Shop Floor SOP. I can confirm that the contents have been explained to me and that I understand the contents. I understand that non-adherence to the above will be managed through the stores disciplinary process.
